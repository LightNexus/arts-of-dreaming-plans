Platform comparison

Arts of Dreaming needs two types of funding infrastructure: a product launch platform for the physical deck, and a recurring membership platform for community and ongoing support. Here's how every major option stacks up.

Recommended — use these
Kickstarter
Physical products
Fees: 5% + 3% + $0.20 per pledge · All-or-nothing model
The best-in-class platform for physical product crowdfunding. Card decks perform extremely well here. All-or-nothing model creates urgency and validates demand before you commit to production. Backers become your first email list. Build your deck campaign here first.
Patreon
Recurring community
Fees: 10% platform + ~3% processing · Monthly recurring
The strongest platform for recurring membership with exclusive content and community identity. Your archetype system makes tiered membership feel natural — each tier deepens the dreamer's practice. Launch alongside or just after Kickstarter.
Indiegogo InDemand
Post-campaign sales
Fees: 5% + 2.9% + $0.30 · Flexible or fixed funding
Less powerful than Kickstarter for initial launch, but the InDemand feature lets you continue taking pre-orders indefinitely after your Kickstarter closes. Run Kickstarter first, then port to Indiegogo InDemand for ongoing deck pre-orders. Secondary use only.
Worth knowing — consider later
BackerKit
Fulfillment-first
Fees: 5% capped at $5/backer
Excellent as a Kickstarter companion tool for pledge management, surveys, and add-ons. Also has its own crowdfunding platform if Kickstarter doesn't fit. Best used as your post-campaign fulfilment layer regardless of which platform you launch on.
Ko-fi
All-in-one shop
Fees: 5% (or $6/mo for 0%) + Stripe processing
Handles tips, one-time product sales, digital downloads, and memberships in one place. Lowest overhead of any option. Good for ongoing deck sales after Kickstarter closes without Shopify fees — or as a lightweight Patreon alternative if you want lower fees.
Crowdfundr
0% platform fee
Fees: 0% platform (supporters cover Stripe fees voluntarily)
Genuinely free and privacy-focused, but requires you to bring your own audience entirely. No discovery built in. Best as a fallback if you want to run a smaller campaign without Kickstarter's all-or-nothing risk — e.g. a limited print run for existing community members only.
Not recommended for this model
Republic
Equity crowdfunding. Only relevant if raising investment capital and willing to give ownership stake. Wrong tool for product launches.
Substack
Newsletter-only. No product commerce, no community features. You're already using Sender.net + Skool which does this better.
Memberful
Monthly platform fees plus transaction fees. Creates overlap with Skool. Worth it only if you outgrow Patreon and need white-label memberships.
Wefunder / SeedInvest
Equity platforms for startup investment rounds. Not designed for product-based creator businesses at this stage.
Plan one

Patreon campaign plan

Patreon is the home for your most committed dreamers — people who want to support the brand beyond one-time purchases. The archetype identity system makes tiered membership feel natural: each tier deepens the practice rather than just adding perks.

Step 1

Membership tier structure

Design first

Four tiers structured around deepening the dream practice — not just adding content volume. Each tier name is an identity the patron can hold.

🌙 The Dreamer
$7/mo
  • Monthly archetype card pull + interpretation
  • Exclusive dream journal prompts
  • Patron-only community feed
  • Early access to new app features
✨ The Oracle
$19/mo
  • Everything in The Dreamer
  • 2 premium Dream Sensei sessions/month
  • Behind-the-scenes: deck art + creation process
  • Monthly archetype deep-dive post
🌟 The Visionary
$39/mo
  • Everything in The Oracle
  • Quarterly exclusive physical card
  • Monthly live dream circle (group session)
  • Skool VIP room access
  • Name in credits for new products
💫 The Luminary
$97/mo
  • Everything in The Visionary
  • Monthly 1:1 dream interpretation (30 min)
  • Founding member recognition
  • Co-creation input on new products
  • First access to all limited physical releases
Pricing rationale & positioning notes

Why these price points

  • $7 is low enough for impulse support but still filters casual followers from genuine community members.
  • $19 is the sweet spot for recurring digital value — equivalent to one premium subscription.
  • $39 with physical mail unlocks a different buyer psychology (tangible product = higher commitment).
  • $97 should feel like a premium coaching relationship, not just content.

Founding member strategy

  • Offer 50 founding spots at 20% off all tiers for the first 30 days.
  • Founding members get a permanent badge and early access to all future tiers.
  • This creates urgency and rewards your most loyal existing users first.
Step 2

Monthly content commitment

Content plan

Patreon succeeds or fails on consistent content delivery. Map the monthly output before you launch so you know what you're committing to.

  • Week 1: Monthly archetype card pull post (all tiers) — the anchor content piece
  • Week 2: Behind-the-scenes post — deck creation, art process, or product development (Oracle+)
  • Week 3: Deep-dive archetype post — rotating through the 9 archetypes quarterly (Oracle+)
  • Week 4: Monthly live dream circle — group video session for Visionary+ patrons
  • Ongoing: 1:1 sessions booked individually for Luminary patrons (calendar link)
  • Monthly: Quarterly physical card mailed to all Visionary+ patrons
Content production notes

Time estimate

  • Weekly post: ~2 hours/post
  • Monthly live session: 1.5 hours inc. prep
  • 1:1 sessions: 30 min each × number of Luminary patrons
  • Physical card fulfillment: outsource to a fulfillment service once Visionary tier has 20+ patrons

Tools needed

  • Zoom or StreamYard for live dream circles
  • Calendly for 1:1 session booking (Luminary)
  • Printful or similar for physical card fulfillment
  • Canva for post graphics and card artwork
Step 3

Pre-launch checklist

Launch sequence

Complete every item before going public. A half-built Patreon page converts poorly and damages first impressions.

  • Write all 4 tier descriptions — identity-driven, not just feature lists
  • Record a 60-90 second welcome video — your face, your voice, your why
  • Create 3 preview posts (free tier visible) showing the quality of patron content
  • Set up Patreon ↔ Discord integration for automatic role assignment per tier
  • Connect Patreon to Zapier → CRM to tag new patrons by tier
  • Prepare founding member email to your existing list (launch day send)
  • Set up fulfillment process for the quarterly Visionary physical card
  • Create 5 weeks of content in advance before going live
  • Test all tier access, payment flows, and cancellation process
Launch day sequence

Day 0 — go live

  • Email blast to full Sender.net list announcing Patreon
  • Post across Instagram, TikTok, and Pinterest
  • Activate founding member 20% discount (50 spots, 30-day limit)
  • Pin Patreon link to all social bios

Goal: Month 1

  • 50+ total patrons across all tiers
  • 10+ at Oracle or above
  • 3+ Luminary founding members
  • Monthly recurring revenue: $500+ MRR
Plan two

Kickstarter campaign plan

The Arts of Dreaming Archetype Card Deck is the perfect Kickstarter product — visually striking, identity-driven, and deeply tied to an existing community that already believes in the archetypes. This plan covers the full 12-week pre-launch runway and 30-day live campaign.

The product

Archetype Card Deck — campaign overview

Flagship launch

A premium 78-card oracle deck with a 180-page hardcover guidebook, housed in a magnetic closure box with gold foil accents. One card per archetype plus supporting dream symbol and practice cards.

Campaign specs

  • Funding goal: $25,000 (covers minimum production run)
  • Campaign duration: 30 days
  • Platform: Kickstarter
  • Fulfillment partner: BackerKit for pledge management
  • Production partner: TBD (recommend MPC or PrintingForLess for cards)

What makes it fundable

  • Existing email list = Day 1 backers before Kickstarter algorithm kicks in
  • Archetype identity = strong social sharing motivation
  • Visual product = strong organic content on TikTok and Instagram
  • Spiritual wellness is a proven Kickstarter category
  • Dream Sensei app = existing user base with built-in investment

12-week runway

Pre-launch timeline

Start 12 weeks out

Kickstarter success is 80% pre-launch. The algorithm rewards campaigns that hit 30% of their goal on Day 1 — which only happens with a warmed-up list and press ready to publish.

1–2
Weeks 1–2
Finalize campaign assets
Lock campaign page copy, headline, reward descriptions. Brief photographer and videographer. Confirm production specs and minimum order quantities with card printer.
3–4
Weeks 3–4
Professional photography & video
Product photography for campaign page and social. Record a 2-minute campaign video. Produce 10 short-form clips (TikTok / Instagram Reels) from the shoot for scheduled release.
5–6
Weeks 5–6
Influencer and press outreach
Identify 20–30 spiritual wellness, tarot, and dream-focused creators on Instagram and TikTok. Send personalised preview decks (physical prototypes). Embargo press for launch week.
7–8
Weeks 7–8
Early backer list building
Launch a dedicated "Notify me" landing page. Run paid social to warm audiences. Goal: 500+ early backer email sign-ups before going live. These are your Day 1 pledges.
9–10
Weeks 9–10
Soft launch to email list
Send early access to your existing Sender.net subscribers — 48-hour head start before public launch. First 100 backers get a special early bird price (-10%). Begin daily social content cadence.
11
Week 11
Countdown and press embargo lift
Daily countdown posts across all channels. Embargo lifts — influencers and press publish their coverage simultaneously for maximum Day 1 spike. Final reminder to notify-me list.
12
Week 12 — Launch day
Kickstarter goes live
Full email blast to entire Sender.net list. All social channels post simultaneously. Live stream on Instagram/TikTok watching the counter. Goal: hit 30% ($7,500) within first 48 hours to trigger Kickstarter's internal algorithm boost.
During the live campaign (30 days)

Keep momentum alive

  • Post backer update every 5 days — show progress, thank community, tease stretch goals
  • Reveal stretch goals one at a time as each is hit — don't show all at once
  • Respond to every comment and question in the first 48 hours
  • Mid-campaign email to non-backers on your list (Day 15)
  • Final 48-hour push email + social to entire audience

Post-campaign flow

  • BackerKit surveys sent within 2 weeks of close
  • All backers added to Sender.net list → Campaign 1 or 2 based on pledge level
  • Port campaign to Indiegogo InDemand for ongoing pre-orders
  • Production timeline communicated to backers within 1 week
Recommended sequence: launch Patreon first to build recurring revenue momentum, then use Patreon patrons as your most vocal Kickstarter Day 1 backers. The two platforms amplify each other — Patreon builds the community that funds the Kickstarter, and the Kickstarter brings in new people who become Patreon patrons.
