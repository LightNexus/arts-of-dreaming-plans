Plan one

Product action plan

This plan recreates the original structure as a clear action framework for improving the app experience, validating the core journey, and preparing the product to support meaningful monetization. The sequence follows the user path from access to insight.

Feature 1

Authentication

Foundation

Goal: Make sign-in feel trustworthy, fast, and predictable so every downstream experience starts cleanly.

→ Unlocks App shell → Unlocks Dream journal
  • Google sign-in works without redirect confusion or dead-end states.
  • Authenticated users land in the right place after login.
  • Sign-out and return sessions behave consistently.
Review details

Tasks

  • Validate Google sign-in, redirect, logout, and refresh flows.
  • Add clear loading and error states for failed authentication.
  • Confirm returning users bypass unnecessary friction.

Dependencies

  • No upstream dependencies.
  • Required before protected content and personalized states.
Feature 2

App shell and navigation

Core UX

Goal: Make the site easy to orient within, so dream, assessment, and report flows feel connected rather than fragmented.

← Depends on Auth → Next: Dream journal → Next: Assessment
  • Primary destinations are immediately understandable.
  • Navigation labels match the user's mental model.
  • Users can move between journal, assessment, and report with confidence.
Review details

Tasks

  • Audit current nav labels and route destinations.
  • Clarify hierarchy between dashboard, dream journal, Dream Sensei, and reports.
  • Add active-state visibility and better empty-state orientation.

Why it matters

  • Clear orientation improves conversion because users understand where value lives.
  • Better flow reduces drop-off before monetized moments appear.
Feature 3

Dream journal

Engagement

Goal: Make journaling feel easy, reflective, and worth returning to.

← Depends on App shell → Feeds Journal Pro upgrade
  • Users can create, revisit, and meaningfully engage with dream entries.
  • Entry creation feels fluid on desktop and mobile.
  • The journal visibly supports the broader dream journey.
Review details

Tasks

  • Test create, edit, save, refresh, and delete behaviors.
  • Improve empty states and prompts for first-time use.
  • Clarify how journal entries connect to insights and future premium features.

Open questions

  • What should be free forever versus part of Journal Pro?
  • How much guidance belongs in the entry flow without slowing it down?
Feature 4

Dreamer type assessment

Identity layer

Goal: Make the assessment feel like a meaningful initiation that produces a believable archetypal result.

← Depends on App shell → Unlocks Archetype report → Feeds Physical deck CTA
  • The assessment is easy to complete and emotionally satisfying.
  • Results feel coherent and personalized.
  • Completion naturally opens the door to the report and next-step offers.
Review details

Tasks

  • Review question flow, pacing, and progress visibility.
  • Test retake behavior and result consistency.
  • Add transitional messaging from assessment into final report.

Business relevance

  • The archetype result is a natural bridge to product offers, email capture, and social sharing.
  • This is one of the strongest identity-based monetization surfaces.
Feature 5

Archetype report

Conversion moment

Goal: Turn the result page into a deeply shareable and revenue-ready destination.

← Depends on Assessment → Feeds Deck CTA, email, share
  • The report feels premium, legible, and emotionally resonant.
  • There is a clear next action rather than a passive ending.
  • The page supports sharing, saving, or buying without breaking tone.
Review details

Tasks

  • Add clear post-report calls to action.
  • Structure the report for skim + depth, not only long-form reading.
  • Introduce deck, email, and social options as aligned extensions of the reading.

Key metric

  • Percentage of completed assessments that continue into a next action.
  • Track CTA clicks, shares, and email capture from this screen.
Feature 6

Settings and profile

Trust

Goal: Give users confidence in account control, preferences, and future subscription management.

← Depends on Auth → Supports Subscription offers
  • Users understand what data and preferences they control.
  • Subscription-ready settings can be added without confusion later.
  • Profile data supports personalization across the experience.
Review details

Tasks

  • Clarify profile, notifications, and saved content controls.
  • Prepare billing and premium-state placeholders even if subscriptions are not live.
  • Reduce ambiguity around account ownership and user history.

Later use

  • This becomes essential when recurring products launch.
  • It also supports retention through personalization and communication preferences.

Priority 1 — Direct product sale

Archetype → Physical Deck CTA

Direct sale

Place the first monetization moment directly after the archetype result, where emotional resonance and identity are strongest.

  • Add a context-aware CTA on the archetype report that positions the physical deck as the embodied companion to the user's archetype.
  • Create specific messaging variants for each archetype so the deck feels chosen, not generically sold.
  • Measure click-through rate, add-to-cart rate, and purchase rate from the report page.
Implementation details

Depends on

  • Archetype report
  • Clean purchase path and product page clarity

Execution notes

  • Use gold accent only around the deck CTA zone.
  • Make the offer feel devotional, tactile, and identity-matched.
2
Priority 2 — Conversion pipeline

Email nurture sequence

Pipeline

Capture interest from users who are not ready to buy immediately, then continue the archetype conversation over time.

  • Add email capture after result delivery and at key reflective moments.
  • Build a sequence that introduces archetype meaning, dream practice, deck relevance, and premium next steps.
  • Segment by archetype so follow-up feels personal and not mass-market.
Implementation details

Depends on

  • Assessment completion
  • Archetype report

Suggested sequence

  • Email 1: Your archetype reading
  • Email 2: How to work with this archetype
  • Email 3: Deck invitation
  • Email 4: Dream Sensei / Journal Pro teaser
3
Priority 3 — Recurring subscription

Dream Sensei freemium gate

Subscription

Offer enough value for users to trust the feature, then gate deeper guidance, history, or premium interpretation behind subscription.

  • Define a generous free layer and a clear premium threshold.
  • Position premium Dream Sensei as ongoing mentorship rather than locked content.
  • Use moments of demonstrated value before showing upgrade prompts.
Implementation details

Depends on

  • Navigation clarity
  • Premium state handling in settings/billing

Premium ideas

  • Longer interpretations
  • History and saved readings
  • Advanced prompts and recurring guidance
4
Priority 4 — Organic growth

Social share card for archetype

Virality

Turn identity into distribution by making the archetype result beautiful and easy to share.

  • Create visual share cards per archetype with a short line of personalized language.
  • Add one-click sharing and copy-link options from the report page.
  • Ensure shared cards route new visitors into the assessment flow.
Implementation details

Depends on

  • Archetype report polish
  • Distinct archetype identity language

Growth focus

  • Design for Instagram story, square post, and link preview formats.
  • Track share rate and downstream assessment starts.
5
Priority 5 — Retention

Daily card pull mechanic

Re-engagement

Give users a simple daily ritual that keeps the site alive between larger milestone experiences.

  • Introduce a once-per-day card draw connected to the dream journey.
  • Use the mechanic to create habit, increase revisit frequency, and support later premium upsells.
  • Connect daily pulls to deck desire, streaks, and email reminders.
Implementation details

Depends on

  • Lightweight home/dashboard return point
  • Basic user history state

Business role

  • Improves retention before asking for bigger commitment.
  • Creates repeated surfaces for premium invitations.
6
Priority 6 — Recurring subscription

Journal Pro upgrade

Subscription

Upgrade the journal from a simple archive into a premium practice space with deeper tools, analysis, and continuity.

  • Reserve premium value for pattern detection, extended prompts, dream tagging, archives, and deeper reflection tools.
  • Introduce the upgrade only after free journaling value has been experienced.
  • Position Journal Pro as the serious practitioner path.
Implementation details

Depends on

  • Dream journal quality
  • Billing-ready settings and premium state UX

Upsell moments

  • After several journal entries
  • When pattern visibility becomes desirable
  • When users want deeper dream history
7
Priority 7 — Retention

Streak system

Retention

Use streaks carefully to support ritual and consistency without making the experience feel gamified in a shallow way.

  • Reward return behavior across journal use, daily pulls, or dream practice actions.
  • Keep the language devotional or reflective rather than aggressively productivity-based.
  • Use streak loss prevention messaging gently and sparingly.
Implementation details

Depends on

  • Daily card pull mechanic
  • Journal usage consistency

Best use

  • Support retention, not primary monetization.
  • Use as a bridge into premium habit-based products later.
8
Priority 8 — Digital product

Courses per archetype

One-time

Create deeper paid learning paths tailored to a user's archetype once identity trust and engagement are already established.

  • Design one course framework, then adapt modules per archetype.
  • Position courses as transformational deep dives rather than informational content bundles.
  • Sell from email, report pages, and premium user journeys.
Implementation details

Depends on

  • Email nurture
  • Clarity around archetype-based curriculum

Offer strategy

  • Use one-time purchase first.
  • Later include inside membership for added perceived value.
9
Priority 9 — Highest LTV

Annual membership bundle

Highest LTV

Build the highest-value offer only after the component products are proven and legible on their own.

  • Bundle Dream Sensei, Journal Pro, daily practice tools, courses, and exclusive member benefits into one coherent annual path.
  • Position membership as the fullest way to live inside the Arts of Dreaming ecosystem.
  • Use this as the premium apex offer for your most engaged users.
Implementation details

Depends on

  • Dream Sensei subscription
  • Journal Pro
  • Courses

Launch condition

  • Only launch once the component offers already convert independently.
  • Bundle should simplify decisions, not hide unclear product architecture.
Recommended execution order: build and polish the archetype report first, because it powers the deck CTA, email capture, and social sharing. Then move into recurring revenue with Dream Sensei and Journal Pro after the core retention loop is in place.

Plan four

Social media plan

A platform-by-platform strategy for turning dream content into community, and community into customers. Each channel has a distinct role — Instagram builds brand trust, TikTok drives discovery, and Skool converts followers into paying members.

Platform 1

Instagram

Brand & Trust

Goal: Build a visually cohesive dream brand that earns trust and drives quiz completions. Instagram is the top-of-funnel for warm leads who are already curious about their inner world.

→ Feeds quiz leads → Feeds email list
  • 3–5 posts per week: archetype reveals, dream symbolism carousels, user testimonials.
  • Stories daily: polls, dream check-ins, behind-the-scenes deck content.
  • Every post has a clear CTA — quiz, app, or email opt-in link in bio.
  • Reels repurposed from TikTok for maximum reach with zero extra effort.
Review details

Content pillars

  • Dream symbolism — What does [symbol] mean? Carousels and quotes.
  • Archetype reveals — "Which dreamer are you?" hooks leading to quiz.
  • Community dreams — User-submitted dreams and interpretations.
  • Product moments — Deck unboxings, app walkthroughs, journal previews.

Key metrics

  • Quiz click-through rate from link in bio.
  • Story reply rate (engagement quality signal).
  • Saves per carousel (reach amplifier).
  • Email list growth attributed to Instagram.
Platform 2

TikTok

Discovery & Reach

Goal: Drive cold audience discovery at scale. TikTok's algorithm rewards dream content — it's a top search destination for people already searching "what does my dream mean."

→ Repurpose to Instagram Reels → Feeds quiz top-of-funnel
  • 3–4 videos per week: dream interpretations, archetype explainers, "POV you dreamed about X."
  • Hook in first 2 seconds — lead with the dream symbol or emotional question.
  • Every video ends with quiz or app CTA in caption and pinned comment.
  • Trend-riding: apply viral audio/formats to dream content weekly.
Review details

Top-performing formats

  • "I dreamed about X" — decode a common dream live on camera.
  • Archetype quiz reveal — "Take the quiz, here's what your type means."
  • Dream series — 3-part breakdowns of recurring dream themes.
  • Deck card pulls — daily card draws tied to dream archetypes.

Key metrics

  • Video completion rate (aim for >40%).
  • Profile visits → link clicks conversion.
  • Comments mentioning personal dream experiences (community signal).
  • Follower growth week-over-week.
Platform 3

Skool community

Conversion & Retention

Goal: Convert social followers and email subscribers into a paying, engaged community. Skool is the home base for the most committed dreamers — where content, connection, and premium access live together.

→ Recurring revenue layer ← Sourced from email list
  • Free tier: weekly dream prompts, archetype discussions, public community wins.
  • Paid tier: live sessions, personalized dream deep-dives, early product access.
  • Weekly post cadence keeps community active between live sessions.
  • Gamification: points for dream journaling, archetype sharing, quiz completions.
Review details

Weekly rhythm

  • Monday — Dream prompt of the week.
  • Wednesday — Archetype spotlight post or member story.
  • Friday — Live session or recorded deep-dive (paid tier).
  • Sunday — Week-in-review + next week teaser.

Key metrics

  • Free → paid tier conversion rate.
  • Weekly active members (WAM).
  • Posts and comments per member per week.
  • Churn rate month-over-month.
Strategy

Cross-platform content system

Operations

Goal: Produce once, distribute everywhere. A unified content calendar and repurposing workflow so the team isn't creating from scratch for each platform each week.

→ Instagram → TikTok → Skool
  • One weekly "hero" piece (TikTok video or long-form post) repurposed into 4–6 assets.
  • Content calendar lives in a shared doc — planned 2 weeks ahead, reviewed weekly.
  • Every social post traces back to a business goal: quiz, email capture, or product page.
  • Monthly analytics review: double down on top formats, cut what doesn't convert.
Review details

Repurposing flow

  • TikTok video → Instagram Reel → YouTube Short.
  • Carousel script → email newsletter section.
  • Community question → Stories poll → blog post seed.
  • Deck card pull → Skool post + email CTA.

Funnel mapping

  • Awareness — TikTok, Instagram Reels.
  • Interest — Instagram carousels, Stories, Skool free tier.
  • Conversion — Quiz → email → app onboarding.
  • Retention — Skool paid, email sequences, Journal Pro.
